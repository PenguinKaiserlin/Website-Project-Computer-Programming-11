Overview of Spain

Spain is a vibrant and diverse destination known for its rich history, stunning architecture, lively culture, and delicious cuisine. From the artistic flair of Barcelona with Gaudí’s masterpieces like La Sagrada Família, to the historic charm of Seville and the royal splendor of Madrid, Spain offers something for every traveler. The country is also famous for its sunny Mediterranean beaches, passionate flamenco music and dance, colorful festivals like La Tomatina and Running of the Bulls, and mouthwatering dishes such as paella and tapas. Whether you're exploring ancient castles, relaxing on the coast, or enjoying late-night strolls through vibrant plazas, Spain promises a warm, unforgettable experience.

Beaches in Spain

Did you know that Spain has over 5,000 miles of coastline and thousands of beaches? Wherever you go, you are never far from a beach. From undiscovered, hidden alcoves to popular tourist resorts, there’s a beach for everyone and with over 300 days of sunshine each year you’re guaranteed the weather to enjoy them! This beautiful country also has the most blue flag beaches in the world so now you’ve no excuse! With thousands of beaches to choose from, where will you go? One of our top suggestions has got to be a holiday to Costa de la Luz. Located in the region of Andalucía, near the Portuguese border, this area is renowned for its beautiful beaches and is a sun worshipper’s paradise!

Food in Spain

For many people, one of the highlights of Spain is, of course, the amazing food on offer. The country’s vibrant cities offer up Michelin-starred restaurants and lively tapas bars, while small-town cafés and restaurants offer up traditional dishes such as paella, seafood stew and chorizo, made with love from family-recipes. Spanish cuisine is known for being delicious, healthy and full of flavour, so there are many unique dishes available that will tempt your taste buds.

Landmarks in Spain

Spain is home to a rich collection of landmarks that reflect its diverse cultural heritage and architectural brilliance. In Barcelona, the iconic Sagrada Família and Park Güell, designed by Antoni Gaudí, showcase unique and colorful creativity. Madrid boasts the grand Royal Palace and world-renowned museums like the Prado Museum. In Granada, the Alhambra stands as a breathtaking example of Islamic architecture, while Seville is home to the majestic Seville Cathedral and the Giralda Tower. Other must-see landmarks include the ancient Roman aqueduct in Segovia, the Cathedral of Santiago de Compostela, and the futuristic City of Arts and Sciences in Valencia. Together, these sites tell the story of Spain’s rich and varied history.
